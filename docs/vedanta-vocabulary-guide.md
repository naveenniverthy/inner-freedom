# Vedanta Vocabulary Guide

This is an internal reference for future writing on Moksha.

Purpose:
- keep public language simple, calm, and human
- keep key Vedanta terms aligned with traditional meanings
- avoid drift into vague spirituality, self-improvement language, or overly academic prose

Source alignment:
- Arsha Vidya UK glossary: https://arshavidya.org.uk/glossary-of-sanskrit-terms.php

General style
- Plain English first.
- Use Sanskrit when it clarifies, not to decorate.
- Avoid translating terms in ways that quietly distort the teaching.
- Prefer short corrections over heavy explanation.
- Do not turn public pages into glossary pages.

## Atma
Preferred public wording
- the Self
- the awareness because of which experience is known

Short explanation
- Atma is the true Self, not the body, mind, or changing personality.

Avoid
- soul
- inner essence as a vague feeling
- a hidden personal self inside the body

Traditional note
- Atma is the ever-present Self, the witness of the changing body-mind complex; not separate from Brahman.

Used on pages
- Future-facing term; not yet foregrounded by name on most public pages.

## Anatma
Preferred public wording
- what is known
- the body, mind, and changing experience

Short explanation
- Anatma refers to what is not the Self.

Avoid
- non-self as a cold abstraction without explanation
- evil, impure, or worthless

Traditional note
- Anatma includes body, mind, senses, and all objects of experience.

Used on pages

## Moksha
Preferred public wording
- freedom
- freedom from taking yourself to be limited

Short explanation
- Moksha is not a future achievement produced by action. It is freedom made clear through self-knowledge.

Avoid
- enlightenment as a special experience
- a reward earned by practice
- spiritual success in the future

Traditional note
- Freedom is already present but covered by ignorance; self-knowledge removes the error.

## Dharma
Preferred public wording
- what is right or appropriate
- what is yours to do

Short explanation
- Dharma here means appropriate action in keeping with the situation, not religion or group identity.

Avoid
- religion
- social conformity
- duty with no nuance

Traditional note
- Dharma includes what is right, appropriate, and in keeping with order and value.

## Karma
Preferred public wording
- action
- action and result

Short explanation
- Karma usually means action, and by extension the result or consequence tied to action.

Avoid
- fate
- cosmic punishment or reward shorthand

Traditional note
- In public pages, spell out whether you mean action, karma yoga, or results of action.

## Self-knowledge
Preferred public wording
- self-knowledge
- understanding who you are

Short explanation

Avoid

- personality optimization

Traditional note
- It is knowledge of the Self, not psychological self-description.

## Sadhana
Preferred public wording
- preparation
- discipline
- preparation of the mind

Short explanation
- Sadhana is not productivity or self-improvement. It is preparation that makes the mind ready for understanding.

Avoid
- hustle
- optimization
- becoming perfect

Traditional note
- Sadhana includes values, steadiness, restraint, and maturity that support knowledge.

## Ishvara
Preferred public wording
- Ishvara, the larger order of life
- Ishvara, the total order

Short explanation
- Ishvara refers to the total intelligent order in which actions and results arise.

Avoid
- vague universe language only
- a merely personal deity unless the page needs that framing
- replacing the term with "energy"

Traditional note
- Ishvara is not just nature as mechanism; it is the total order, including laws, intelligence, and results.

## Prasada Buddhi
Preferred public wording
- acceptance
- receiving what comes as coming through a larger order

Short explanation
- Prasada Buddhi is the attitude of receiving results with composure rather than resistance.

Avoid
- passive resignation
- liking everything that happens

Traditional note
- It is a karma-yoga attitude toward results, not passivity.

## Ishvara Arpana Buddhi
Preferred public wording
- offering
- offering action into Ishvara, the larger order of life

Short explanation
- This is the attitude of dedicating action, while not demanding ownership over results.

Avoid
- giving up effort
- fatalism
- vague surrender without action

Traditional note
- It is part of karma yoga: wholehearted action offered into the total order.

## Viveka
Preferred public wording
- discernment
- seeing what is lasting and what is changing

Short explanation
- Viveka is discrimination that helps the mind stop mistaking the changing for the truly dependable.

Avoid
- cleverness
- overthinking

Traditional note
- More precisely, viveka is discrimination between the lasting and the non-lasting.

Used on pages
- [discipline](/Users/naveensmacbook/Desktop/inner-freedom-site/app/discipline/page.jsx)

## Vairagya
Preferred public wording
- dispassion
- non-dependence on changing things

Short explanation
- Vairagya means freedom from leaning on the changing for lasting completeness.

Avoid
- indifference
- emotional numbness
- withdrawal from life

Traditional note
- Not hatred of the world, but freedom from dependence on it for fulfillment.

Used on pages
- [discipline](/Users/naveensmacbook/Desktop/inner-freedom-site/app/discipline/page.jsx)

## Maya
Preferred public wording
- Usually avoid on beginner-facing pages unless needed.

Short explanation
- If introduced later, explain carefully as the power because of which the world appears and is understood in Vedanta, not as simple illusion or magic.

Avoid
- "the world is fake"
- simulation language

Traditional note
- Maya and avidya should not be flattened into one vague idea.

Used on pages
- Not currently foregrounded.

## Satya
Preferred public wording
- what does not depend on something else for its reality

Short explanation
- Use only when the page truly needs it. Add nuance.

Avoid
- simply "real"

Traditional note
- Satya is not just "physically real"; it is that which is not negated and does not depend on another.

Used on pages
- Not currently foregrounded.

## Mithya
Preferred public wording
- dependent reality
- not independent in itself

Short explanation
- If used publicly, explain that mithya is not nothing; it depends on something else.

Avoid
- unreal
- imaginary
- false in the sense of total nonexistence

Traditional note
- Mithya is neither absolutely real nor absolutely nonexistent.

Used on pages
- Not currently foregrounded, but relevant to [snake-rope](/Users/naveensmacbook/Desktop/inner-freedom-site/app/clarity/snake-rope/page.jsx)

## Avidya
Preferred public wording
- ignorance
- self-misunderstanding

Short explanation
- Avidya is not mere lack of data. It is ignorance opposed to clear knowledge.

Avoid
- simple forgetfulness
- low intelligence

Traditional note
- Avidya is overcome by knowledge, not by action alone.

Used on pages
- Implied on [clarity](/Users/naveensmacbook/Desktop/inner-freedom-site/app/clarity/page.jsx), [snake-rope](/Users/naveensmacbook/Desktop/inner-freedom-site/app/clarity/snake-rope/page.jsx)

## Seer / Seen
Preferred public wording
- seer and seen
- the knower and what is known

Short explanation
- Use this to distinguish awareness from objects of experience.

Avoid
- "seer" as a special mystical person
- observer as an ego standing apart from life

Traditional note
- In dṛg-dṛśya viveka, the final seer is awareness itself.
